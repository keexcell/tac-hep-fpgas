Because latency is how quickly a process can get done, the scheduler is dependent on latency to tell it how often it can schedule tasks.

If you give a maximum latency constraint lower than the natural latency (by that I mean the latency that the code would have without any constraints/scheduling done), then the scheduler is going to have to assign tasks to different workers/use more resources to get the job done in less time.
If the minimum latency constraint is higher than the natural latency, the scheduler would extend out the tasks to meet the latency minimum.
